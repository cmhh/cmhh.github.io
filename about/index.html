About this blog

I work for a National Statistics Office as a methodologist, which invariably involves a fair bit of computer programming and the like. It’s interesting work, and so I find myself tinkering with similar things in my free time.

This blog mostly contains posts describing things I’ve tried that I find interesting. But this also means that more often than not I am describing tools that could serve free, and perhaps easier to use or more productive alternatives to the proprietary tools I use professionally. The posts themselves are mostly just a convenient way of sharing what I’ve tried with colleagues, but maybe others will find something here useful…

Just a collection of things I find interesting...
